PS 211: Introduction to Experimental Design

Fall 2025 · Section C1 · Instructor: Kate Nussenbaum

COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course is an introduction to the logic and processes involved in descriptive and inferential statistics for psychology. Topics include frequencies, measures of central tendency and variability, statistical inference, significance, t-tests, ANOVAs, correlation, regression, chi-square tests, and statistical software analysis.

PREREQUISITE

General Psychology (PS 101). The course will make use of both the programming language R and the interface RStudio, but no prior programming experience or knowledge of R is expected.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • By the end of this course, students should understand:
  • Different types of variables and how to measure them.
  • The difference between populations and samples, and the basic concept of sampling.
  • Different types of descriptive and inferential statistics common in psychological research.
  • The concept of hypothesis-testing.
  • How to use R to conduct basic statistical analyses of data.
  • How to use R to create visualizations of data.
  • How to report and interpret the results of inferential statistical tests.
